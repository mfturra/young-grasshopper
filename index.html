Loan Expenses

Due to the high cost of a college education, typically students need to take out a student loan to pay for their yearly costs. Use the following section to calculate the projected cost of your loan considering different interest rates.

What type of loan are you considering?

Going to college is a big decision! It's totally understandable that you're undecided. Prior to providing you with a loan to pay for your tuition, banks use your families credit score and your own to estimate your likelihood to pay the full balance. That's why you'll find a range of values online.

Fixed rate means that your interest rate will stay fixed until you finish paying off your loan. So your monthly payments will remain unchanged.

Variable rate means that your interest rate will change for any given month. Your monthly payments could increase or decrease until you finish paying off your loan.

Researching Expected Wage Based on Occupation

Prior to starting your occupation, you can actually find out how much you could expect to get paid. Use the following steps to calculate your projected annual income.

Use the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics website to see what the mean, or average, hourly rate and annual, or yearly wage, is based on the occupation.

Navigating Occupation Website

Below you'll find two paths. The first is a white collar occupation, the second is a blue collar occupation. Click on either path to better understand how much your occupation could make per hour and annually in the region where you live. Having two tabs open are going to be helpful for you to follow the next steps.

For this example we'll get information on a Software Developer. This market is set to grow at 25% over the next 10 years, which is much faster than the national average.

Use the elements below to store the values that you find during your research.

Occupation Title

National Annual Salary

Regional Annual Salary

  • Click on the link to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics website.
  • Click on the Computer and Mathematical Occupations major group category. Navigate to the 15-1252 Software Developers sub-category and click the corresponding link.
  • Scroll down to the first table. Jot down the Mean Annual Wage that you find in the table above. This is the occupations national average annual salary.
  • Now we're going to review the areas with the highest employment level in this occuption. Use Ctl+F or Command+F option to search for "Metropolitan areas with the highest employment level in Software Developers".
  • Jot down the Regional Mean Annual Wage for this occupation using the closest Metropolitan Area to your location in the table above. Notice that depending on where you live, your annual salary could be greater or less than the national average.
