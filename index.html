Loan Expenses

Due to the high cost of a college education, typically students need to take out a student loan to pay for their yearly costs. Use the following section to calculate the projected cost of your loan considering different interest rates.

What type of loan are you considering?

Going to college is a big decision! It's totally understandable that you're undecided. Prior to providing you with a loan to pay for your tuition, banks use your families credit score and your own to estimate your likelihood to pay the full balance. That's why you'll find a range of values online.

Fixed rate means that your interest rate will stay fixed until you finish paying off your loan. So your monthly payments will remain unchanged.

Variable rate means that your interest rate will change for any given month. Your monthly payments could increase or decrease until you finish paying off your loan.

Researching Expected Wage Based on Occupation

Prior to starting your career, it can be helpful to get a general understanding of how much you could be getting paid to work in a specific occupation. Use the following steps to calculate your projected income.

On a yearly basis, the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics updates the salaries of occupations across the country. They provide users with information on the mean (or average) hourly rate and annual (or yearly) wage, based on the occupation.

Choose Your Own Journey

Time for you to learn how to research what your salary could be depending on the occupation that you pursue. There are currently three journeys below. The first two will provide you with guidance on how to find specific information on how much those occupations make annually based on national public resources. It's recommended that you go down one of those two paths first so that you can get a feel for how to collect the information that you're looking for.

The last path involves acquiring more information on the occupation that you're pursuing so that you can compare you're expected annual salary versus your future actual annual salary.

For this example we'll get information on a Software Developer. According to the BLS, they create computer applications that allow users to do specific tasks and the underlying systems that run the devices and control networks. This market is set to grow at 25% over the next 10 years, which is much faster than the national average.

  • If you were pursuing a Software Engineering degree, an occupation that you could pursue is one as a Software Developer. Click on the link to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics website.
  • Click on the Computer and Mathematical Occupations major group category. The major group are then broken up into sub-categories. Navigate to 15-1252 Software Developers and click the corresponding link.
  • Scroll down to the first table. Jot down the Mean Annual Wage that you find in the table above. This is the occupations national average annual salary.
  • Now we're going to review the areas with the highest employment level in this occuption. Use Ctl+F or Command+F option to search for "Metropolitan areas with the highest employment level in Software Developers".
  • Jot down the Regional Mean Annual Wage for this occupation using the closest Metropolitan Area to your location in the table above.
